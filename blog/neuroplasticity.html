Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Neuroplasticity

Understanding the principles of neuroplasticity has far-reaching benefits for people. It reinforces the idea that our reality is often a reflection of our beliefs or expectations. If you believe that your mind is fixed, then your mind will be fixed. If you believe that your mind is limitless, that you can learn anything with a bit of work, that you can improve, alter and build your brain and mind to become anything that you want it to be, then this also will become true.

I have engaged with the principles of neuroplasticity for several years, at least. Once I understood that my brain was malleable, I started to let go of some assumptions that I had about myself. I began embracing the fact that I could improve characteristics of myself that I thought were flawed. I’ve done this through reading, intentional practice and meditation. It’s interesting how we can just think about doing something and still show improvement in it without physically doing it. I’ve utilized this in my piano practice where I’ll imagine myself playing a particular piece if I don’t have a piano nearby. When I finally do sit down at a piano, the piece feels just as fresh as if I had been practicing it regularly. I hope to use this with programming by trying to consciously think about the concepts even when I’m not actively studying.

Growth Mindset

Growth mindset is the understanding that our minds are not fixed, that they are malleable. And by recognizing the principles of neuroplasticity, and utilizing these principles in learning, our mind becomes unhindered by the limitations of a fixed mindset. With a growth mindset, our ability to grow and learn new skills through hard work becomes apparent: we aren’t limited by the flaws that we see in ourselves. This is relevant because Our limitations are only imagined. With a growth mindset, you can learn anything, do anything, become anything you imagine if you want it enough.

I have been and will continue to integrate a growth mindset into my learning journey by ignoring any inner dialogue that tells me I’m incapable of doing something. In the front of my mind I’ll keep believing that I can change my brain, I can change my skillsets, I can learn anything I want to.

Blending Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset With My Learning Plan

I’ve been utilizing my understanding of neuroplasticity and growth mindset for quite a while and will continue to do so throughout my learning journey. A big part of really leaning into these concepts is building habits. For me, the habit is just sitting down and doing the work every day to understand what I need to know in order to progress to the next level on understanding programming. It’s the repetition and continued exposure to challenging concepts that will force the brain to generate new neural pathways and turn those spindly little connections into superhighways of knowledge over time. By building and maintaining the habit to continuously DO THE WORK, and with the power of a growth mindset to give me hope that I will understand these things eventually, neuroplasticity will follow, and my brain will change and my skill and ability will grow over time.
