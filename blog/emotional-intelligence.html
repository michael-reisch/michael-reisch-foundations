Emotional Intelligence

What is emotional intelligence?

The capacity to be aware of, control, and express one’s emotions, and to handle interpersonal relationships judiciously and empathetically. “Emotional intelligence is the key to both personal and professional success.”

Signs of Emotional Intelligence:

  • An ability to identify and describe what people are feeling.
  • An awareness of personal strengths and limitations.
  • Self-confidence and self-acceptance.
  • The ability to let go of mistakes.
  • An ability to accept and embrace change.
  • A strong sense of curiosity, particularly about other people.
  • Feelings of empathy and concern for others.
  • Showing sensitivity to the feelings of other people.
  • Accepting responsibility for mistakes.
  • The ability to manage emotions in difficult situations.

Components of Emotional Intelligence:

  1. Perceiving emotions – The first step in understanding emotions. This can include understanding body language and facial expressions.
  2. Reasoning with emotions – Using emotions to promote thinking and cognitive activity, prioritizing what we pay attention to.
  3. Understanding emotions – How we interpret the source of the emotions of others.
  4. Managing emotions – Regulating emotions and responding appropriately.

How is Emotional Intelligence Different from Intellectual Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence involves your ability to regulate your own emotions as well as how to interact with and perceive others. With a high EQ, you can objectively shift awareness to your own emotional state and the emotional state of others with whom you interact. By having a high EQ, you can more effectively work with others towards a common goal by avoiding misunderstandings arising from a lack of emotional awareness that could lead to miscommunications and emotional outbursts.

Why is Emotional Intelligence Important?

Emotional intelligence is important because it acts as a way for smooth collaboration. If all collaborators have a keen awareness of the emotional state of themselves and their peers, then they can operate from a place of empathy and understanding. Thus, the team will be more effective at achieving their goals than if the team-members only had high IQ. You could have a room of the most intelligent people in the world to create a technological breakthrough, but if they don’t have adequate EQ, the team is less likely to achieve their goal, and if they do, it may not reach its highest potential.
